# Students’ Social Media Addiction Analysis – Education & Behavioral Insights

## Executive Summary
Schools and policymakers faced concerns about declining student well-being and performance due to excessive social media usage. This project analyzed survey data from **705 students** to identify patterns between screen time, addiction, sleep, and mental health.  

**Key Insights:**  
- Students with **high addiction scores (≥9/10)** scored **50% lower** in mental health.  
- Excessive daily screen time (>4 hours) correlates with **50% decrease** in sleep hours and negative academic outcomes.  
- Adequate sleep (7–8 hours) improves mental health scores by **50-60%**.  

**Impact:** Provided actionable recommendations to improve student mental health and academic performance, enabling data-driven interventions.  

---

## Business Problem
Educational institutions are increasingly concerned about **student mental health, academic performance, and social media addiction**. Without quantitative insights, interventions are inefficient, and resources may be misallocated.  

**Problem Statement:**  
- Students with high social media usage risk poor sleep and low mental health scores.  
- There is limited understanding of the **specific relationship between screen time, addiction, and outcomes**.  
- Institutions need actionable insights to **inform policies, programs, and student support**.

## Recommendations
1. **Limit social media usage:** ≤4 hours/day for students  
2. **Promote healthy sleep:** Encourage 7–8 hours per night  
3. **Target interventions:** Monitor students with addiction score ≥7  
4. **Awareness programs:** Educate students and parents on screen time and mental health impact  

---
